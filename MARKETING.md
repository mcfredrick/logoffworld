# People Over Tech — Marketing Strategy
*One person. ~1 hour/day max. Designed around the product's built-in viral mechanics.*

---

## Core Principle

The product markets itself if the share loop works. Every dollar of effort goes into:
1. Making the share moment irresistible (brag cards, milestone reactions)
2. Placing the site in front of people who are already looking for it (targeted communities)
3. Getting trusted voices to endorse it (charities, podcasters, journalists)

---

## Daily Routine (~15 min)

- Post today's prompt across active channels with the day's brag card image
- Reply to any comments, shares, or DMs
- Check Plausible for any unusual referral traffic spikes — investigate and engage the source

**Suggested post copy template:**
> Today's prompt: *"[prompt text]"*
> ☁️ [connection count] found their sky. 🥁 [rebel count] marched their own drum.
> Free, ephemeral, no accounts. → peopleover.tech
> #PeopleOverTech #DigitalMinimalism #SlowLiving

---

## Weekly Rhythm (~3 sessions of 10–15 min)

**Monday — Community post**
Rotate through these communities, one per week. Don't spam. Contribute genuinely.
- r/nosurf *(perfect audience — anti-doomscroll ethos)*
- r/digitalminimalism
- r/minimalism
- r/anticonsumption
- r/slatestarcodex *(intellectually curious, will appreciate the philosophy)*
- r/simpleliving
- Hacker News "Show HN" *(one-time launch post, then monthly check-ins)*
- Bluesky `#nosurf` and `#digitalminimalism` tags

**Wednesday — Engagement**
- Like and comment on 5–10 posts from accounts in the digital minimalism / slow living space
- Follow back anyone who shares the site
- Repost/quote any organic shares with a genuine response

**Friday — Bigger content**
Rotate through:
- Charity spotlight: "This month we're supporting [Charity]. Here's why." + link to their work
- Behind the scenes: how the site works, why it resets daily, what the data means
- Community milestone: "X humans paused this week" with a reflection
- Prompt of the week: expand on why a particular prompt resonated

---

## Monthly (~1 hour total across the month)

**Charity partnership email** (template, personalise per charity):
> Hi [Name], I run People Over Tech — a daily site where people pause from tech to reflect on one human prompt. This month's charity is [Charity]. [X] people paused in your name this month.
> Would you be willing to share it with your newsletter / social following? Happy to provide copy and the brag card image.

This is the highest-leverage channel. A single charity newsletter mention can drive thousands of users.

**Podcast / media pitch** (1–2 per month, short list below):
- The Idler (UK — perfect philosophical fit)
- Your Undivided Attention (Center for Humane Technology — directly aligned)
- The Minimalists Podcast
- Offline (digital wellness podcast)
- Note to Self / IRL (tech + humanity)
- Tech Won't Save Us
- Any local / indie podcast that covers mindfulness, slow living, or digital wellness

Pitch angle: *"I built a site that resets to zero every day — no history, no streaks, no accounts. It donates to charity. Here's why."*

**Transparency update** — post the monthly numbers publicly: visits, votes, funds raised, donated. This builds trust and gives charities and sponsors confidence.

## Built-In Viral Mechanics (product does the work)

| Feature | How it spreads |
|---|---|
| Brag card | Users share after voting — each share is a word-of-mouth impression |
| Milestone reactions | "You were the 1,000th human to pause today" — screenshot-worthy, shareable |
| Holiday themes | Seasonal relevance drives periodic spikes |
| Ephemeral reset | Creates daily FOMO — "come back tomorrow" |
| Charity tie-in | Charities share to their own audiences when they're featured |
| Sponsored prompts | Sponsors have an incentive to share (their name is on it) |

---

## Launch Sequence (Week 2)

1. **Day 1** — Personal network: share with friends, family, existing followers. Ask for honest feedback.
2. **Day 2** — Post to r/nosurf with a genuine "I built this because..." story post (not a link drop)
3. **Day 3** — Hacker News "Show HN" — title: *"Show HN: I built a daily site that resets to zero every night and donates to charity"*
4. **Day 4** — Product Hunt launch (schedule for a Tuesday or Wednesday, 12:01am PT)
5. **Day 5** — Email the Month 1 charity (Crisis Text Line) with the first week's numbers
6. **Day 7** — Recap post: "One week in. Here's what happened." — share real numbers publicly

---

## What NOT to Do

- No paid ads until there's clear organic traction (waste of money pre-product-market-fit)
- No growth hacking or engagement bait — it contradicts the brand
- No posting the same content to every platform on the same day — feels spammy, gets ignored
- No vanity metrics — follower count doesn't matter; referral traffic and votes do
- Don't chase virality — build genuine community in 2–3 places rather than spreading thin across 10
